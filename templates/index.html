🧘 Simple Desk Yoga & Stretches (For Sitters)

Simple physical releases to execute directly in your study chair every 2 hours

1
Seated Neck & Shoulder Release

Drop your right ear to your right shoulder, gently holding with your right hand for 15s. Repeat on the left. Roll shoulders backwards 5 times to release tension from looking down at papers.

2
Seated Spinal Twist

Sit straight. Place your right hand on your left knee, and left hand behind your chair. Gently twist left, looking over your shoulder. Breathe for 3 deep cycles, then twist right.

3
Desk Wrist & Forearm Flex

Extend your right arm forward, palm facing out, fingers down. Use left hand to gently pull fingers back for 15s. Shake wrists out to prevent strain from writing answers.

⚡ Cognitive Efficiency & Free-Time Learning Suggestions

Study methods backed by cognitive neuroscience to learn smart, not hard

🔄 Spaced Repetition System (SRS)

Review lessons at expanding intervals (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14). This transitions concepts into long-term memory, preventing last-minute exam cram panic.

📝 Active Recall Retrieval

Instead of re-reading pages passively, close the book and write down everything you remember. Actively forcing retrieval creates much stronger neural paths.

💡 The Feynman Explanation Method

Explain a complex syllabus topic in simple, plain language as if explaining to a 10-year-old child. If you get stuck, it immediately exposes your exact conceptual gaps.
